Buying Guide: How to Choose Office Furniture

Choosing the right office furniture depends on your workspace, budget, and ergonomic needs. Here are the key factors to consider before making a purchase.

EXPERT TIP

Measure your space first. Many buyers overlook dimensions. Always measure your room height, desk area, and door clearance before ordering large furniture pieces. Allow at least 36" of walking space around your desk.

1. Standing Desks: Height Range & Stability

Look for a minimum height range of 28"?C48" to accommodate both sitting and standing positions. Dual-motor systems are quieter and lift faster than single-motor. Check weight capacity — most quality desks support 250?C350 lbs.

2. Office Chairs: Lumbar Support & Adjustability

An ergonomic chair should have adjustable seat height (16?C21"), lumbar support, and tilt tension. Mesh backs are breathable for long hours. Look for a 5-star base and at least 2" of seat depth adjustment.

3. Filing Cabinets: Locking & Fire Rating

For home offices, a 2-drawer file cabinet is usually sufficient. Look for central locking, ball-bearing drawer slides, and if storing important documents, choose a model with a fire rating of at least 30 minutes (Class 350).

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best height for a standing desk

Your elbows should form a 90— angle when typing. Most desks adjust from 28" to 48". The exact height depends on your height — a 6' person typically uses 42?C44" standing, 28?C30" sitting.

How much should I spend on an office chair

A good ergonomic chair starts around $200?C$300. Budget chairs ($100?C$200) work for occasional use. For 8+ hour daily use, invest $400+ in chairs from brands like Herman Miller, Steelcase, or Haworth.

Are standing desks worth it

Yes — research shows alternating between sitting and standing improves posture, reduces back pain, and may increase productivity. However, standing all day is not recommended either. The key is to alternate every 30?C60 minutes.
